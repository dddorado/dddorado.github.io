About

Academic profile

Dan Anthony Dorado is a full-time faculty member at the UP School of Library and Information Studies. His work connects library and information science with quantitative methods, digital scholarship, information systems, and public-facing academic engagement.

Biography

At UP SLIS, he teaches information technology, management and marketing, research methodology, and quantitative research. He previously served as Director of the Diliman Learning Resource Center under the Office of the Vice Chancellor for Student Affairs and, before joining the faculty, worked as Information Specialist at the UP College of Engineering Library with responsibility for system and network administration and The Learning Commons.

He completed his Master of Technology Management at UP Diliman and is currently pursuing a PhD in Data Science. His research specialization, as described in the resume, covers computational methods, open education, critical data studies, and radical statistics, with related work in AI in libraries, tourism analytics, digital humanities, and research data management.
